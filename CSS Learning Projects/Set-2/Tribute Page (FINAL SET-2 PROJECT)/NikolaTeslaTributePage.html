Nikola Tesla

The man who electrified the world

Here's a timeline of Nikola Tesla

  • 1856 -Born in Smiljan, Austrian Empire (present-day Croatia)
  • 1875 -Enrolls at the Austrian Polytechnic in Graz
  • 1878 -Moves to Budapest to attend the Technical University
  • 1880 -Moves to Prague to continue his studies
  • 1881 -Begins working for the Continental Edison Company in Paris
  • 1884 -Arrives in New York City with little money but a letter of recommendation addressed to Thomas Edison
  • 1885 -Begins working for Thomas Edison's company in New York City
  • 1887 -Establishes the Tesla Electric Company and begins working on alternating current (AC) electrical systems
  • 1888 -Develops the concept of the induction motor and AC transmission system
  • 1891 -Constructs the first Tesla coil
  • 1893 -Demonstrates wireless energy transmission at the World's Columbian Exposition in Chicago
  • 1895 -Begins experiments with X-rays
  • 1899 -Constructs the Wardenclyffe Tower in New York to experiment with wireless communication and energy transmission
  • 1900 -Files numerous patents for inventions related to electrical engineering and radio technology
  • 1901 -Experiments with terrestrial stationary waves
  • 1917 -Receives the Edison Medal for his contributions to electrical engineering
  • 1943 -Dies in New York City at trong
